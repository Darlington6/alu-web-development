What is Public Speaking?

Public Speaking is an oral presentation or speech delivered to a live audience. It is generally a formal or staged event, although impromptu speeches are common and can be a defining career moment.

Types of Public Speaking.

There are many types of Public Speaking, including Informative, Persuasive, Entertaining, and Demonstrative. Unlike Informative Speaking, which conveys information, in Persuasive Speaking, the Speaker aims to convince the audience of something. Entertaining Speaking focuses on entertaining the audience, while Demonstrative Speaking involves showing how something is done.

How do people perceive public speaking?

People perceive public speaking differently, often influenced by their experiences and personalities.

Many people feel anxious about speaking in public. The fear of being judged, making mistakes, or forgetting what to say can be overwhelming. Some view public speaking as an opportunity to share ideas, inspire others, and create positive change. They feel empowered by the chance to communicate effectively.
